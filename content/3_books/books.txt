Title: Books

----

Text:

This curated collection features books I've enjoyed reading, own, or regularly use as reference materials, organized by topic. Originally compiled as a resource for my students, I hope you'll find these recommendations valuable for your own learning journey.

**Design:**
(link: https://www.setmargins.press/books/what-design-cant-do/ text: What Design Can’t Do)
Essays on Design and Disillusion
Silvio Lorusso

(link: https://www.lars-mueller-publishers.com/designing-design?srsltid=AfmBOop_KsS1fSFUgIlnWo-qglwfBun7ZjU1bycjIDbJTSPr2r4g2dG5 text: Designing Design)
by Kenya Hara

(link: https://www.lars-mueller-publishers.com/super-normal?srsltid=AfmBOopREISMxvrq61m5fmR3xpe6eK4IypnJCVjglxYh-5Haj0qH-UfG text: Super Normal)
by Naoto Fukasawa and Jasper Morrison

(link: https://www.phaidon.com/products/dieter-rams-as-little-design-as-possible?srsltid=AfmBOopfUW0KrtD-TRrdX94COvGVqGWKw7o1VWoRQLcTEZtR2TvNZOxW text: Dieter Rams: As Little Design as Possible)
by Sophie Lovell

(link: https://worksthatwork.com text: Works that work 1-10)
by Peter Bilak

(link: https://www.lars-mueller-publishers.com/blind-maps-and-blue-dots?srsltid=AfmBOopNz9nuUwUKFygQf-fDp9U8bN8iZzdHvYijRrps8mkulVjz3mWP text: Blind Maps and Blue Dots
)The Blurring of the Producer-User Divide in the Production of Visual Information
by Joost Grootens

(link: https://valiz.nl/en/publications/caps-lock text: CAPS LOCK)

by Ruben Pater

**Coding:**

By Daniel Shiffman

by Demian Conrad, Rob van Leijsen, David Bennewith, Max Bruinsma, Ian Lynam, David Cabianca, and others

(link: https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RR295.html text: Internet Freedom and Political Space)
by by Olesya Tkacheva, Lowell H. Schwartz, Martin C. Libicki, Julie E. Taylor, Jeffrey Martini, Caroline Baxter, and others

(link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.5555/1196347 text: Designing Interactions (Extracts))
by Bill Moggridge
